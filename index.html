Richard Spikes

The holder of a number of United States patents

Here's a time line of Richard Spike's life:

  • 1908- Invented a new and useful Improvement in BeerTappers
  • 1910- Invented certain new and useful Improvements in Billiard-Cue Racks
  • 1919- Invented certain new and useful Improvements in Take-up Devices
  • 1923- Invented certain new and useful Improvements in of which the Brake-Testing Machines
  • 1923- Invented a device commonly known as a-pantograph and used in connection with electric railroad cars
  • 1926- Invention relates to improvements in milk bottle openers and contemplates the disclosure of an opener
  • 1932- Invention relates to apparatus for sampling liquids stored in tanks, particularly oil in large storage tanks, andthe objects of the invention are to provide-apparatus by which an average sample of the liquid may be obtained and/or the average temperature of the liquid
  • 1932- Invention relates to improvements in automatic gear shifts and it consists 'of the combinations, constructions and arrangements hereinafter described and claimed.
  • 1933- Invention relates to improvements in transmissions and shifting means
  • 1950- Invention relates to improvements in a seat for a barbers chair, and "its principal object is to provide a convenient seat for a barber working on a customer to relieve fatigue and to prevent the development of varicose veins to which-barbers are frequently subject-because of continuous standing for "long working hours.
  • 1962- Invention relates to improvements in a braking system, and more particularly to braking systems for wheeled vehicles which provide a reserve braking action in case of damage to the normal braking means.
